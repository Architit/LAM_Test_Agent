# STRUCTURAL SYSTEMS MAP V1

## Purpose
Define separate sovereign structural systems for full lifecycle orchestration.

## Systems Registry
1. `FormationSystem`
- mission: frame intent, boundaries, resource envelopes.
- outputs: formation scope, base constraints, initial invariants.

2. `ConceptSystem`
- mission: generate and normalize concept vectors.
- outputs: concept models, hypothesis set, scenario branches.

3. `DesignSystem`
- mission: materialize architecture and interfaces.
- outputs: topology design, contracts, protocol templates.

4. `ImplementationSystem`
- mission: produce executable artifacts from contracts.
- outputs: code modules, services, test harnesses.

5. `EmbodimentSystem`
- mission: operationalize and deploy.
- outputs: runtime activation profile, deployment evidence, observability anchors.

6. `ImaginationSystem`
- mission: expand possibility search-space.
- outputs: alternative evolution vectors and speculative branches.

7. `CreationSystem`
- mission: assemble coherent creative composites.
- outputs: compositional prototypes and draft structures.

8. `GenerationSystem`
- mission: produce scalable candidate variants.
- outputs: generated options, candidate packs, diversity matrix.

9. `SelectionSystem`
- mission: filter by safety, utility, and viability.
- outputs: accepted/rejected sets, rationale ledger, promotion queue.

10. `SynthesisSystem`
- mission: merge approved branches into resilient whole.
- outputs: integrated synthesis state, dependency reconciliation report.

11. `CreativationSystem`
- mission: convert abstract creative vectors into implementable directives.
- outputs: creative-directive bridge contracts.

12. `HibernationSystem`
- mission: freeze low-priority contours safely.
- outputs: hibernation snapshots, wake triggers, cold-state descriptors.

13. `StasisSystem`
- mission: preserve stabilized states under minimal mutation.
- outputs: stasis locks, integrity heartbeat, reactivation gates.

## Inter-System Relation
- Systems are independent structures connected via contracts and gates.
- Cross-system transition requires:
  1. declared source and target system IDs,
  2. contract reference,
  3. safety gate result,
  4. audit event record.

## Minimal Transition Contract
- `from_system`
- `to_system`
- `contract_id`
- `safety_gate_status`
- `operator_or_agent_id`
- `timestamp_utc`
